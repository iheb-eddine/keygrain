    Turn on in-page autofill?

    Keygrain can show a small fill button right inside the login boxes on the sites you've saved — so you can fill your username and password without opening this popup.

    To do that, your browser will ask permission for Keygrain to run on websites. Here's the honest version:

    • The fill button only appears on sites you've saved in Keygrain. Keygrain does nothing on any other site.
    • Nothing leaves your device. Keygrain still creates your passwords right here on your computer, and only when you pick an account.
    • Your master secret is never entered on web pages — only here in the Keygrain popup.
    • You can turn this off anytime in Settings, and the permission is removed with it.

    Next, your browser will show its own permission prompt. It may say something like "read and change your data on all websites" — that's your browser's standard wording for this kind of permission. Keygrain only ever acts on the sites you've saved.
