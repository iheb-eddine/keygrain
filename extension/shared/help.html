🔑 Keygrain Help

Frequently asked questions

What is Keygrain?

Keygrain is a deterministic password manager. Instead of storing passwords, it generates them on the fly from your master secret + email + site name.

The same inputs always produce the same password. Nothing is stored, nothing can be stolen from a server breach.

Getting started

1. Enter your email address.

2. Choose a strong master secret (passphrase). This is the only thing you need to remember.

3. Add services (websites) — Keygrain generates a unique password for each one instantly.

What is my master secret?

Your master secret is the single passphrase that generates all your passwords. It is never stored anywhere — not on your device, not on any server.

⚠️ If you lose your master secret, all your passwords are lost. There is no recovery mechanism. This is by design.

The colored dots (fingerprint) help you verify you typed it correctly — same secret always shows the same colors.

How does sync work?

Sync stores your service list (names, sites, emails, options) encrypted on the server. The encryption key is derived from your master secret + email — the server never sees your plaintext data.

Your master secret and generated passwords are never transmitted.

How do I rotate a password?

Edit the service and click 🔄 Rotate password. This increments an internal counter, generating a completely new password.

After rotating, update the password on the actual website. Click ✅ Mark as rotated once done.

How do I migrate from another manager?

Menu → Migrate from another manager. Export a CSV from your old password manager and import it into Keygrain.

Keygrain will map entries to its format. You can then update each site to use the Keygrain-generated password at your own pace.

What is the PIN for?

The PIN provides quick unlock without retyping your full master secret. It encrypts your secret locally on the device.

The PIN does not replace your master secret. If you clear browser data, you'll need your master secret again.

Keyboard shortcuts

Ctrl+Shift+K (Cmd+Shift+K on Mac) — Auto-fill credentials on the current site.

↓ / ↑ — Navigate the service list.

Enter — Fill the selected service.

Escape — Clear search or close the popup.

What if I forget my secret?

You cannot recover it. There is no reset, no backup, no support email that can help. This is by design — nobody can access your passwords except you.

Recommendation: write your master secret on paper and store it somewhere physically secure (e.g., a safe). Never store it digitally.

Troubleshooting

Wrong password generated? Check that the email and site name match exactly what you used when you created the entry. Sites are case-insensitive, but verify the exact domain matches.

Sync failing? Verify the server URL in Settings and ensure you're using the same email you synced with originally.

Auto-fill not working? Some sites block auto-fill. Try the right-click context menu, or use the copy button in the popup and paste manually.
